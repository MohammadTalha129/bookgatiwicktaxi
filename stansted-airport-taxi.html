How long is a taxi from Gatwick to Stansted? (LGW to STN Journey Details)

Gatwick Airport (LGW) and Stansted Airport (STN) are roughly 71–75 miles (114–121 km) apart by road. The M25 and M11 motorways are primarily used for this long-distance transfer.

Essential Travel Buffer: We strongly advise giving any traveler who needs a connecting flight at least 3.5 to 4 hours to complete the process, including airport check-in, possible traffic delays, and collection.

Off-Peak Travel (Midday, Mid-Morning):

  • 75 to 90 minutes is the estimated transfer time (just for the journey).
  • Suggested Allocation: Allocate two hours, including collection and buffer.

Peak Times: 7-9 a.m. and 4-7 p.m

  • 95 to 120 minutes is the estimated transfer time (just for the journey).
  • Suggested Allocation: Allocate two hours and thirty minutes, including collection and buffer.

Late in the evening (11 p.m. to 5 a.m.):

  • Travel-only estimated transfer time: 70 to 85 minutes
  • Suggested Allocation: Allocate one hour and forty-five minutes, including collection and buffer.

Stansted Airport Facts & Pickup Guide

With more than 29 million passengers passing through each year, Stansted (STN) is the fourth busiest airport in London. It streamlines the pickup process by operating from a single terminal, unlike Gatwick.

Stansted (STN) Pick-Up Protocol (For your return STN to LGW booking)

  • Stansted Airport Single Terminal: Your driver will meet you inside the main Arrivals Hall, usually near the information desk or a big store, and will be holding a sign with your name on it.

Gatwick (LGW) Pick-Up Protocol

  • We cover both the North Terminal and the South Terminal. Your driver will meet you in the terminal's Arrivals Hall and take you to the M25.
